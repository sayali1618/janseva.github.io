An educated youth from village Ulup in Bhum taluka of Osmanabad district of Maharashtra has started Janseva Gramin Vikas Sanstha in Year 1997, was established for the purpose of development and upliftment of living standards in rural areas.
This organization has been duly and properly registered under Act 1860 dated 14/10/1997 with certificate number MAHA 349/1997 Osmanabad and under Act 1950 dated 30/07/1998 as per F-3666 Osmanabad.
The organization's PAN, TAN, and Policy Commission registrations, e-grants, all these registrations are done on the legally appropriate portal. This Organization has been doing enormous amount of work in rural area of Maharashtra since establishment.



Janseva Sanstha was formed in the year 1997-2000 by bringing together the youth of Ulup, Bramhanpur, Rameshwar, Chincholi, Warud villages through Nehru Youth Center Osmanabad in each village.

The youth of this place were given guidance on youth leadership camps and youth skills, business education and youth rights through the Youth Development Center.
The unit was sanctioned by the Government of India's Youth Development Center. The economic and social development of the youth is done by the organization through this unit.



About Us Page

Under the scheme of Golden Jubilee Village Self-Employment Scheme, through Zilla Parishad Osmanabad, District Rural Development Agency Osmanabad had appointed Janseva Gramin Vikas Sanstha Ulup in the year 2000 for Bhum and Paranda taluka. The organization was allotted 34 villages in Bhum taluka and 21 villages in Paranda taluka for its work. Through the organization 1500 Self Help Groups have been established in Bhum and Paranda taluka and 15000 women have been well connected in the concept of Self Help Group. The organization has developed women through Self Help GroupS with a view to inculcating business skills. The women have received financial assistance from various banks. Through this financial assistance, women are now able to stand on their own feet and are engaged in dairy business, goat rearing and various small-scale industries. About 15,000 women have got permanent business. They have a good knowledge of the market as well. Special Component Scheme: - Janseva Gramin Vikas Sanstha and Mahila Arthik Vikas Mandal Mumbai jointly under the Special Component Scheme in Osmanabad district. Self Help Groups have been set up in the Scheduled Tribes and the organization has imparted training to 500 women on various topics such as business skills and functional literacy and has started projects like dairy business to help women stand on their own feet. They have also been provided loans of Rs 1.5 to 2 lakh from NABARD and nationalized banks.
